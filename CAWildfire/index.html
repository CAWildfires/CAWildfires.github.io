Active Fire and Fire Risk Maps

Comparing fires in Northern California, the Central Valley, and Southern California, fires have historically been concentrated in Northern California and the Central Valley. Northern California saw much less rainfall in 2020 compared to Southern California. The ravaging fires and high temperatures are a reminder of the effects of climate change. Additional factors that contribute to these fires are extremely dry seasons, high winds, and lightning.

Why It’s Important To Know Fire Hazard Zones: Protect yourself and know your fire hazard zones! In areas where the map indicates orange to red, it is very important to make sure you and your community are prepared in the event of a fire.

Why It’s Important to Know Areas Of Active Fires And Evacuation Zones: Know your evacuation zones! In the event of a wildfire, it is important to be updated on the changing evacuation zones so that you know when it is most appropriate to evacuate.

Research on Respiratory Health Outcomes

Wildfire smoke is extremely harmful to lungs, especially for children, older adults and those with asthma, COPD and bronchitis or a chronic heart disease or diabetes.

Particle pollution triggers asthma attacks, heart attacks and strokes. Studies of children in California found that children who breathed in smoky air had more coughing, wheezing, bronchitis, colds, and were more likely to have to go to the doctor or to the hospital for respiratory causes, especially from asthma.

Forest fire smoke is a colorless, odorless gas called carbon monoxide (CO). Inhaling CO reduces oxygen delivery to the body's organs and tissues and can lead to headaches, nausea, dizziness and, in high concentrations, premature death.

Wildfires spread many other harmful emissions, including nitrogen oxides and many hazardous air pollutants.

Fire Safety, Prevention, and Mitigation

Wildfires impact environmental sustainability but also affect economic and societal sustainability. Wildfires have burned about 1.8 million acres in California this year in 2021, according to Cal Fire. The fires have destroyed nearly 3,000 structures. Residents of South Lake Tahoe evacuated their residence in August because of the Caldor Fire.Low income communities, migrant populations, indigenous populations, communities of older people, and POC are most affected by the problem of worsening wildfires.

Recommended Masks: an N-95 or N-100 filter will filter out the damaging fine particles

Home Care: Make sure your fire and carbon monoxide detectors are working properly and you know where the fire extinguishers and fire escape are located. Use air conditioners on the recirculation setting to keep from pulling outside air into the room. Air cleaning devices that have HEPA filters can provide added protection from the soot and smoke.

Contact

Fluctuating temperatures and increased wildfires in California cause poor air quality and pose a threat to communities and environmental sustainability. Wildfires threaten the Air Quality Index (AQI) to move to dangerous levels and affect the daily lives of citizens in health and socioeconomic factors. In an environmental sustainability perspective, wildfires have significantly increased amounts of greenhouse gases and burned 1.8 million acres in California in the past year. Additionally, a large fire in 2020 killed 31 people, and destroyed nearly 2500 structures and buildings.
